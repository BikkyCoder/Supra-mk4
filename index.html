The Legend - Supra MK4 2JZ-GTE Motor: Engine Specifications & Power Heritage

Heritage

A Journey Through Automotive Excellence: 1993-2002 Toyota Supra Overview

Born in 1993, the fourth-generation Toyota Supra (A80/MKIV/MK4) emerged as more than just a sports car—it became an iconic platform that would define an era of Japanese automotive excellence and JDM culture, powered by the most legendary engine ever built: the 2JZ-GTE.

Legendary Supra MK4 2JZ-GTE Engine: 320 HP Motor with Unlimited Tuning Potential

The 2JZ-GTE Engine is the heart and soul of the Supra MK4—a 3.0-liter (2,997cc) inline-six masterpiece with 320 horsepower stock and 315 lb-ft of torque. Equipped with sequential twin turbochargers, this MK4 Supra motor represents the pinnacle of 1990s performance engineering. The 2JZ-GTE wasn't just built for impressive stock performance; it was deliberately overengineered to handle immense power upgrades reaching 1000+ HP, making the Supra MK4 engine one of the most reliable and tuneable high-performance motors ever created. Whether you want 450 HP Stage 1 tuning, 600 HP Stage 2 modifications, or 1200+ HP Stage 3 builds, the Toyota Supra MK4 2JZ delivers.

Supra MK4 Performance: Stock Specifications & Motor Capabilities

Supra MK4 specifications are impressive from the factory: 320 HP and 315 lb-ft of torque from the 2,997cc displacement. The stock Supra MK4 engine delivers a 0-60 time of 4.6 seconds and a top speed of 155 mph, exceptional for 1993. Connected to a 6-speed Getrag manual transmission, the MK4 Supra offers razor-sharp performance. But this Toyota Supra MK4 motor is truly special because it's built with heavy-duty internals designed for massive boost—the factory engineers knew tuners would push it hard, so the 2JZ-GTE was created with reinforced pistons, forged crankshaft, and robust cylinder head to safely handle enormous power outputs.

Racing Heritage & Top Speed Records

From dominating the Japanese Grand Touring Car Championship (JGTC) to setting records at time attack events worldwide, the MK4 Supra proved its racing pedigree. Its top speed success in motorsports further cemented its status as a true driver's car with 155 mph limiter.

Cultural Impact - Fast and Furious Legacy

The MK4 Supra transcended beyond automotive culture, becoming a star in movies and popular media. Its appearance in "The Fast and the Furious" franchise with Paul Walker's iconic Supra introduced it to a new generation of enthusiasts, creating an enduring street racing legend.
