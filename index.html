The Legendary JDM Icon That Redefined Performance

The Toyota Supra MK4 (A80) isn't just a car—it's a cultural phenomenon that has dominated the automotive world since 1993. Whether you're a JDM enthusiast, a tuner, or simply appreciate automotive excellence, the fourth-generation Supra represents the pinnacle of Japanese engineering.

Why the 2JZ-GTE Engine is Legendary

At the heart of every turbocharged Supra MK4 beats the immortal 2JZ-GTE inline-six engine. This 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged powerhouse produces 321 horsepower in stock form, but its true genius lies in its bulletproof construction. The 2JZ-GTE features a cast-iron closed-deck block, forged steel crankshaft, and robust internals—deliberately over-engineered by Toyota to handle extreme power. This is why the 2JZ can reliably produce 1,000+ HP with the right modifications.

Key 2JZ-GTE Engine Features

  • Cast-iron block with closed-deck design for maximum rigidity
  • Forged steel crankshaft rated for extreme boost
  • Sequential twin-turbo system (CT20 turbochargers) at 11 PSI stock
  • Getrag V160 6-speed manual transmission (virtually indestructible)
  • 0-60 acceleration: 4.6 seconds stock, sub-3 seconds with modifications
  • Top speed: 155 mph (electronically limited), 200+ mph unlocked

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Supra MK4 0-60 time?

The stock Toyota Supra MK4 accelerates from 0-60 mph in 4.6 seconds with the 6-speed manual transmission. The automatic version is slightly slower at 4.9 seconds. Modified Supra MK4s with 1000+ HP can achieve sub-3 second 0-60 times.

How much horsepower does a stock Supra MK4 have?

The stock 2JZ-GTE engine produces 321 horsepower at 5,600 RPM and 315 lb-ft of torque at 4,000 RPM for USDM models. Japanese market models were rated at 280 HP due to the gentleman's agreement.

Can a Supra MK4 reliably make 1000 HP?

Yes, absolutely. The 2JZ-GTE is famous for handling 1000+ HP reliably. The engine features a cast-iron closed-deck block, forged crankshaft, and robust internals. Thousands of Supra MK4s worldwide run 1000+ HP with proven durability over millions of miles.

What is the Supra MK4 top speed?

Stock Supra MK4s are electronically limited to 155 mph (250 km/h). With the limiter removed and proper modifications, Supra MK4s can exceed 200+ mph depending on power level and aerodynamic setup.

Is the Supra MK4 better than the MK5?

Stock performance: MK5 (382 HP) is faster than MK4 (321 HP). Tuning potential: MK4 wins decisively with 1200+ HP capability vs MK5's ~500 HP limit. Value: MK4 offers superior modification potential and aftermarket support built over 30+ years.
