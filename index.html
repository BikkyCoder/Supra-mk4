The Legend

Heritage

A Journey Through Automotive Excellence

Born in 1993, the fourth-generation Toyota Supra (A80) emerged as more than just a sports carâ€”it became an icon that would define an era of Japanese automotive excellence.

Engineering Marvel

At its heart beats the legendary 2JZ-GTE engineâ€”a 3.0-liter inline-six masterpiece equipped with sequential twin turbochargers. This powerplant wasn't just built for performance; it was overengineered to handle immense power upgrades, making it one of the most reliable high-performance engines ever created.

Design Philosophy

The MK4 Supra's design was revolutionary for its time. The flowing lines, distinctive headlights, and that iconic rear wing created a silhouette that remains timeless. Chief Engineer Isao Tsuzuki's vision was to create a car that could compete with European exotics while maintaining Japanese reliability.

Racing Heritage

From dominating the Japanese Grand Touring Car Championship (JGTC) to setting records at time attack events worldwide, the MK4 Supra proved its racing pedigree. Its success in motorsports further cemented its status as a true driver's car.

Cultural Impact

The MK4 Supra transcended beyond automotive culture, becoming a star in movies, video games, and popular media. Its appearance in "The Fast and the Furious" franchise introduced it to a new generation of enthusiasts, creating an enduring legacy that continues to inspire.
