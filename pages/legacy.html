Evolution Timeline

Tracing the iconic history of the Supra across generations

1978
Celica Supra Born
The first generation Celica Supra was introduced as a performance variant of the Toyota Celica, marking the beginning of an automotive legend.
1986
A60 Series Supra
The second generation introduced the legendary 7M-GE engine and established Supra as a true performance legend in the sports car segment.
1993
A80 MK4 Revolution
The iconic MK4 Supra debuted with the legendary twin-turbocharged 2JZ-GTE engine, producing 320 horsepower and 315 lb-ft of torque.
1997
2JZ-GTE Enhanced
Mid-cycle refresh increased power to 330 hp while maintaining the iconic twin-turbo character that made the MK4 legendary.
2002
Final Generation
The MK4 Supra production ended, leaving behind a legacy of performance, reliability, and cultural impact that endures to this day.
Today
Living Legacy
The MK4 Supra remains one of the most coveted performance cars ever built, with thriving communities and continued modifications worldwide.
