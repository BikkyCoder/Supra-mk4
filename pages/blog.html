Supra MK4 FAQ: Facts, Pricing & Specifications

Common questions about MK4 Supra for sale, MSRP, production numbers, wallpapers, and complete history

The original MSRP for the 1993 Supra MK4 started at approximately $36,000-$40,000 USD, varying by trim level and year. By 2002 (final year), prices had increased to $45,000+. Current 2021 market values range from $70,000-$150,000+ depending on mileage, condition, and modifications. The 320 HP naturally aspirated model commanded the highest prices.

The MK4 Supra offered multiple trim levels including Base, Sport Roof, and Turbo variants. Total production numbers from 1993-2002 exceeded 17,000 units worldwide. RHD (right-hand drive) versions were produced for Japan and other markets. Color production numbers vary, with white and black being most common. Production peaked in mid-1990s before declining through 2002 final year.

The naturally aspirated MK4 Supra produces 320 HP and 315 lb-ft torque from the 2JZ-GE engine (NA model). The turbocharged 2JZ-GTE version produced 320 HP stock but is capable of 1200+ HP with proper tuning. Stock 0-60 time is 4.6 seconds with a top speed of 155 mph (electronically limited). With modifications, performance increases dramatically.

A VIN decoder for MK4 Supra helps identify production year, manufacturing location, engine type, color code, trim level, and options. The 10th character indicates model year (1993-2002). Use online VIN decoders or Toyota documentation to reveal color production numbers, RHD/LHD configuration, and exact specifications for your vehicle.
